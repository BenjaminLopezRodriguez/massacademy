# Mass Academy — Product Marketing Context

## Product

Mass Academy is an institution where people who've mastered a craft turn decades of professional judgment into companies. Not a school, accelerator, or incubator in the traditional sense.

**Core job to be done:** "Help me turn what I know from years of practice into something that outlasts any single job."

## Audience

People who've mastered a trade in the broadest sense:

- Electricians who know the work
- Lawyers who know compliance
- Therapists who know psychology
- Nurses, accountants, teachers, welders, operators, etc.

**Identity:** "I know things that can't be found online." They are practitioners, not aspiring founders reading Hacker News.

**Skepticism to overcome:** Tech platforms that talk down to them, ask them to learn to code, or treat expertise as content to be scraped.

## Launch stage

**Phase 2–3: Alpha / Beta.** Landing page live, community rooms functional, applications captured in-product. Not open self-serve incubation yet.

## Owned channel strategy (ORB)

| Channel | Role |
|---------|------|
| Website + rooms | Primary owned asset. Capture expertise, route to field-specific rooms. |
| Applications DB | Owned waitlist. Every applicant is a relationship, not a mailto. |
| Email | Not yet built. Next priority after room engagement. |

Rented (LinkedIn, etc.) should drive to the expertise prompt, not the homepage generically.

## Core product loop

```
Enter expertise → Join field room → Create profile → Share intent
       ↓                                              ↓
  Classify craft                              See peers' work
       ↓                                              ↓
  Unity + social proof                    Apply to incubator
```

### Psychology mapped to product

| Step | Model | Implementation |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Expertise prompt | Activation energy, foot-in-the-door | One field, one click. No account required. |
| Profile sheet | IKEA effect, commitment & consistency | Name + craft + bio = invested member |
| Intent field | Goal-gradient, Zeigarnik | "What are you working on?" creates open loop |
| Room directory | Unity principle, social proof | "People like me in my field" |
| Getting started checklist | Goal-gradient effect | 3 steps, strikethrough on completion |
| Apply form | Reciprocity, regret aversion | Low-friction, personal follow-up promise |

## Community identity

**Shared identity:** Practitioners who know what the manual doesn't say.

**Core ritual (weekly, future):** "What are you working on this week?" in each room.

**Member value:**

1. Peers in the same field, not a generic forum
2. Visibility into what others are building
3. Path to incubator for serious builders

## North star metric (launch)

**New members who post intent within 7 days of joining a room.**

Secondary: room member count, application submissions, profile completion rate.

## Voice

- Quiet confidence. First principles. No buzzwords.
- Never: AI-powered, revolutionary, disruptive, next generation.
- Speak to craft, not startup culture.

## What we are NOT

- A coding bootcamp
- A generic founder community
- A content marketing play
- An AI tool demo
